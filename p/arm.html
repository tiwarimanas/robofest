Application

The Robotic Arm project showcases the capabilities of a mechanical arm controlled by electronics and programming. This versatile system is capable of performing various tasks, such as lifting, gripping, and moving objects with precision. Designed for educational and practical purposes, the robotic arm serves as a platform for learning about robotics, automation, and engineering principles.

System Behind

The project consists of a mechanical arm with multiple joints and servo motors, controlled by an Arduino microcontroller. The microcontroller receives commands from a user interface, which may include buttons, joysticks, or even gesture recognition sensors. Based on these commands, the microcontroller sends signals to the servo motors, enabling precise movement of the robotic arm. This integration of hardware and software allows for flexible and programmable operation of the robotic arm, making it suitable for a wide range of applications.
