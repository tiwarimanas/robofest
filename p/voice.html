Voice Control Car Project

From..

Aniket Sharma, Nikhil Tiwari and Shourya Mishra - Class 9

Application

The Voice Control Car project introduces an innovative way to interact with a car using voice commands. By incorporating a Bluetooth module, the car can establish a wireless connection with a smartphone. This allows users to control the car's movement simply by speaking commands into their phone, offering a hands-free and intuitive driving experience. The project showcases the potential of voice recognition technology in the field of robotics and automation.

System Behind

At the core of the Voice Control Car project is a Bluetooth module that facilitates communication between the car and a smartphone. The car's microcontroller, often based on Arduino, receives voice commands transmitted via Bluetooth and translates them into corresponding actions, such as forward, backward, left, and right movements. This seamless integration of voice control and robotics demonstrates the feasibility of hands-free interaction in various practical applications, from home automation to entertainment.
